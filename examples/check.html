"In the presence of a network partition, a distributed system must choose between consistency and availability."

— CAP Theorem

CA
CA Databases (Consistency + Availability)
  • Examples: Traditional RDBMS (PostgreSQL, MySQL, Oracle)
  • Transaction safety with ACID properties
  • Only works when network partitions don't occur
  • Limitation: Not truly distributed; sacrifices partition tolerance
CP
CP Databases (Consistency + Partition Tolerance)
  • Examples: MongoDB, HBase, Google BigTable, Redis
  • Strong consistency even during network partitions
  • Will refuse writes to maintain data integrity
  • Trade-off: May become unavailable during partitions
AP
AP Databases (Availability + Partition Tolerance)
  • Examples: Cassandra, DynamoDB, CouchDB
  • Always available for reads and writes, even during partitions
  • Uses eventual consistency model
  • Trade-off: May return stale or conflicting data
CAP Theorem in Databases • October 9, 2025
Network partitions are inevitable in distributed systems, forcing the CP or AP choice
